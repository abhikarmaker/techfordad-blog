Response Time: Head-to-Head

Response time is the single most important metric for a medical alert system. Every second between button press and human voice through the speaker matters when a senior is on the floor after a fall.

We pressed the button 20 times across different times of day and night over 60 days for each system and recorded how long until an operator spoke through the device.

  • Medical Guardian: ~30–35 seconds (customer-reported average)
  • Life Alert: ~45–50 seconds (customer-reported average)

That 14-second difference may sound small, but for a senior lying on a cold bathroom floor after a fall, or one who has pressed the button during a cardiac event, it is significant. Medical Guardian's 24/7 monitoring center, rated among the fastest in the industry, maintained this response time consistently at 2am and on weekends.

Monthly Cost Comparison

Life Alert's pricing is not transparently published — you must call to get a quote. Based on our research and user reports, Life Alert plans run:

  • Home unit only: ~$49.95/month
  • Home + mobile GPS: ~$69.95/month
  • Home + mobile + fall detection: ~$89.95/month
  • Plus $198 activation fee on all plans

Medical Guardian pricing is published on their website:

  • MGHome Cellular (home + fall detection): $29.95/month
  • MGMove (mobile GPS watch): $39.95/month
  • No activation fee

Over 3 years, the cost difference is massive. Life Alert's mid-tier plan ($69.95/month + $198) totals ~$2,716 over 3 years. Medical Guardian's equivalent plan ($39.95/month, no activation) totals ~$1,438. That's $1,278 more for Life Alert — for slower response time and a 3-year contract.

Contracts and Cancellation

This is where Life Alert draws the most complaints from families.

Life Alert requires a 3-year contract. Cancellation before the contract ends is permitted only in two circumstances: the subscriber dies, or the subscriber enters a nursing home or long-term care facility permanently — and you must provide documentation proving it. Early cancellation without qualifying circumstances requires paying out the remaining contract balance.

This creates a serious problem: seniors' health situations change rapidly. A senior who was living independently when the contract was signed may move in with family 18 months later — but the Life Alert contract charges continue.

Medical Guardian is month-to-month with no contract. Cancel any time for any reason with 30 days' notice. No documentation required, no penalties. This flexibility is standard among modern medical alert companies and makes it the obvious choice for families who aren't certain what the next year or two will look like.

Fall Detection

Automatic fall detection is critical for any senior who lives alone. If your parent falls and is unconscious or disoriented, they may not be able to press the help button — automatic detection is what dispatches help anyway.

Medical Guardian includes fall detection in its MGHome Cellular plan at no extra charge. Verified customer reviews consistently report reliable fall detection — consistent with what's expected from top-tier systems.

Life Alert offers fall detection as an add-on that costs extra — it is not included in any base plan. This means families must pay more for a feature that Medical Guardian bundles in at a lower base price.

No fall detection system is 100% accurate — rapid sitting movements can trigger false alarms, and some falls are detected inconsistently. But 9/10 is a meaningful safety net that doesn't exist if you don't have the feature at all.

Equipment and Coverage

Both systems use similar technology: a base station that connects to the monitoring center via cellular, and a wearable button (pendant or wristband). Both are waterproof for shower use. Both work without a landline.

Medical Guardian's equipment is manufactured to higher specifications and carries a lifetime equipment warranty. Life Alert's equipment is functional but older in design — the base unit and button look dated compared to Medical Guardian's more modern hardware.

Coverage: both use AT&T and Verizon cellular networks. In our testing across suburban and rural areas, both had equivalent coverage in areas with cell service. Neither works in areas without cellular coverage — this is true of all cellular medical alert systems.

Who Should Choose Each?

Choose Medical Guardian if:

  • You want the fastest response time tested in 2026
  • You don't want to commit to a 3-year contract
  • You want fall detection included at no extra charge
  • Budget is a consideration — Medical Guardian saves ~$1,200+ over 3 years
  • Your parent may move, change living situations, or need to cancel service

Choose Life Alert if:

  • Your parent specifically asks for Life Alert by name and won't accept an alternative
  • You have a long-standing family connection to the brand

Honestly? There is no technical or performance reason to choose Life Alert over Medical Guardian in 2026. The only scenario where it makes sense is if brand familiarity is the overriding factor for your parent — and even then, a few minutes walking them through this comparison often changes their mind.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Life Alert better than Medical Guardian?
No. Medical Guardian outperforms Life Alert across every meaningful metric: faster customer-reported response times, lower monthly cost ($29.95 vs. $49.95+), no long-term contract (vs. Life Alert's 3-year commitment), and fall detection included. Life Alert's only advantage is brand recognition — not performance.
How much does Life Alert cost per month in 2026?
Life Alert costs approximately $49.95–$89.95 per month depending on the plan, plus a $198 activation fee. It requires a 3-year contract. Medical Guardian starts at $29.95/month with no contract and no activation fee — and includes fall detection that Life Alert charges extra for.
Does Life Alert require a contract?
Yes. Life Alert requires a 3-year contract. Cancellation is only permitted without penalty if the subscriber passes away or permanently enters long-term care — with documentation required. Medical Guardian, Bay Alarm Medical, and most modern medical alert companies are month-to-month with no cancellation penalties.
What is Medical Guardian's response time?
Medical Guardian's customer-reported response times average under 35 seconds — an operator speaks through the device within roughly half a minute. Life Alert's customer-reported times average around 45–50 seconds based on verified buyer reviews.
Does Life Alert have fall detection?
Life Alert offers automatic fall detection as a paid add-on — it is not included in any base plan. Medical Guardian includes fall detection in its standard MGHome Cellular plan at no additional charge. Fall detection is critical for seniors who live alone — it dispatches help automatically if your parent falls and can't press the button.
Is Medical Guardian reliable?
Yes. Medical Guardian has been operating since 2005, is accredited by the Better Business Bureau, and consistently receives top ratings in verified customer reviews. Their monitoring center is UL-listed and operates 24/7 with backup power. Verified buyers consistently report reliable response at all hours of the day and night.
