Choosing a medical alert system in Canada is different from the US market. Services like Life Alert don't operate here, and Canada has its own strong options — including the non-profit MedicAlert Foundation and Telus LivingWell, backed by one of Canada's largest telecoms.

We researched every major medical alert service available across Canadian provinces, comparing monthly costs in CAD, monitoring centre locations, fall detection accuracy, and coverage in both urban and rural areas.

1. MedicAlert Canada — Most Trusted Overall

MedicAlert Foundation Canada

From ~$30 CAD/month + membership

  • Canada's most recognized medical alert brand
  • Non-profit — funds go back into service quality
  • Includes medical ID jewellery with your health info
  • 24/7 Canadian-staffed monitoring centre
  • Works with provincial health authorities in emergencies

MedicAlert Canada is unique in the medical alert space — it's a registered non-profit charity that has been operating in Canada since 1961. Unlike US-based for-profit companies, MedicAlert includes a medical ID bracelet or necklace engraved with your critical health information (allergies, medications, conditions), which first responders are trained to look for.

The 24/7 monitoring centre is staffed by Canadian agents who can communicate with provincial 911 systems and provide your full medical profile to paramedics. For seniors with complex medical histories, this integration is genuinely life-saving.

Canada Medical Alert Buying Guide

Key differences from US systems

  • No Life Alert in Canada — the heavily-advertised US brand doesn't operate here
  • Carrier coverage matters more — cellular-based units should run on a major Canadian network (Telus, Rogers, Bell) for rural reliability
  • Landline requirements — many older home base stations require a landline; cellular-only options are better for Canadians who've cut the cord
  • Bilingual monitoring — if your parent is French-speaking, confirm the monitoring centre offers service en français

Questions to ask before signing up

  • Is the monitoring centre based in Canada?
  • Which cellular network does the mobile unit run on?
  • Is there a long-term contract, and what are the cancellation terms?
  • Does the service work in my province's rural areas?
  • Is fall detection automatic or does it require pressing the button?

Provincial Subsidies & Assistance Programs

Unlike the US where Medicare rarely covers medical alerts, several Canadian provinces offer partial or full subsidies for eligible seniors:

  • Ontario — Assistive Devices Program (ADP) and some LHIN home care contracts cover certain systems
  • British Columbia — BC's Home and Community Care program sometimes subsidizes Lifeline
  • Alberta — Continuing Care programs may assist with costs for eligible seniors
  • Quebec — RAMQ and CISSS programs offer assistance in some regions

Contact your provincial health authority or a local seniors' centre to find out what's available in your region before paying full price. Many families are surprised by what's covered.

Our Recommendation for Most Canadian Families

Start with MedicAlert Canada if your parent has complex medical conditions — the medical ID and Canadian monitoring integration are unmatched. Choose Telus LivingWell if rural coverage is a concern or if your parent is active and needs GPS. If your parent already has an iPhone and is tech-comfortable, the Apple Watch SE avoids monthly fees entirely.

Always check with your province first — you may qualify for a subsidy that makes the decision easier.
