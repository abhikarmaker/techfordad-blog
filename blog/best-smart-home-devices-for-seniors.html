Smart home technology can genuinely improve quality of life and safety for seniors — but not all of it. A lot of "smart home" products make life more complicated, not less. We focused our testing on devices that pass a simple test: would a 78-year-old with no prior smart home experience actually use this every day?

The answer turns out to be very selective. Here are the 7 devices that passed the test.

The Echo Show 8 is the single most impactful smart home device for seniors. It sits on a kitchen counter or bedside table, always on, always listening, and lets your parent video call family, play music, check weather, set medication reminders, and control lights — all without touching a phone or navigating any menus. Just talking.

Essential voice commands to teach your parent:

  • "Alexa, call [Name]" — video call family
  • "Alexa, remind me to take my pill at 8am"

  • "Alexa, turn on the lights"
  • "Alexa, what time is it?"

Setup tip: Set this up for your parent. Add yourself and other family members to their Alexa contacts. Enable Drop In (with their knowledge) so you can check in when needed.

Falls are the #1 cause of injury death in adults over 65 — and many happen at night when seniors get up and fumble for a light switch. Smart bulbs and plugs eliminate this hazard: set a schedule to dim bathroom and hallway lights to 20% between midnight and 6am, so there's always soft light without the harsh brightness that disrupts sleep.

Priority rooms: Bedroom, bathroom, and any hallways between them. Install smart bulbs (not smart plugs) in ceiling fixtures. Use smart plugs on bedside lamps for voice control.

Frequently Asked Questions

What smart home devices are best for elderly people?
The 5 most impactful: (1) Amazon Echo Show 8 — voice-controlled video calls, reminders, and music; (2) Ring Video Doorbell — see who's at the door without getting up; (3) Smart plugs and bulbs — voice-controlled lighting eliminates reaching for switches; (4) Smart thermostat — maintains comfortable temperature automatically; (5) Robot vacuum — eliminates bending and heavy vacuuming. These cover safety, comfort, and connection at reasonable cost.
Can Alexa call 911 for a senior?
Alexa cannot call 911 directly in the US. However, Alexa can call family members by voice, connect to trained emergency responders via Alexa Together's Urgent Response feature, and Drop In so you can check on your parent. For actual emergency SOS, pair the Echo Show with a dedicated medical alert system or Apple Watch SE.
Is Alexa good for seniors with dementia?
For early-to-moderate dementia: yes, with specific benefits — medication reminders, playing familiar music (research-backed benefit for dementia patients), answering simple questions, and controlling lights by voice. For moderate-to-advanced dementia: Alexa Together or a caregiver-managed setup works better. Alexa is not a substitute for dementia care, but it's a useful daily living aid.
What is the best way to prevent falls at home for seniors using technology?
Smart home fall prevention: (1) Motion-sensor night lights in hallways and bathrooms — turn on automatically in the dark; (2) Voice-controlled smart lights — no fumbling for switches; (3) Smart plugs to eliminate extension cord tripping hazards; (4) Ring doorbell so your parent doesn't rush to the door. Beyond tech: remove throw rugs, install grab bars, ensure all areas are well-lit.
Can I monitor my elderly parent at home with smart devices?
Yes — Ring indoor cameras, Echo Show Drop In, and Alexa Together's Activity Feed all allow family check-ins. Smart plugs can serve as indirect routine monitors (did the coffee maker turn on this morning?). Always discuss monitoring with your parent beforehand — their privacy and dignity should guide what you set up, and transparent monitoring is far better for the relationship than hidden surveillance.
