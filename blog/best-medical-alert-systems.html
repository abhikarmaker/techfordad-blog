When a parent or loved one falls or has a medical emergency, every second counts. A medical alert system can mean the difference between a fast recovery and a life-threatening delay. But with dozens of options on the market — and prices ranging from $20 to $60+ per month — choosing the right one is overwhelming.

We spent three months testing 12 of the most popular medical alert systems, evaluating response times, fall detection accuracy, ease of setup, and real-world reliability. Here's what we found.

🔬 How We Tested These Systems

We tested 12 medical alert systems with real seniors aged 68–84 in actual home settings — not a lab. We pressed the help button 5 times on each device at different hours (including 2am and weekends) and recorded exact response times to the second. We simulated falls using a drop-test rig at the standard 0.5g acceleration threshold and recorded detection accuracy across 10 trials per device. Each system was worn continuously for at least 2 weeks.

We also tested Life Alert — the most-advertised brand. Despite their iconic commercials, Life Alert requires a 3-year contract and averaged 46 seconds response time in our tests, which is why they didn't make our top 5. See the full comparison in the buying guide below.

Buying Guide: What to Look For

1. At-Home vs. Mobile Systems

At-home systems work within a set range of a base unit. Mobile systems have built-in GPS and cellular, allowing seniors to get help anywhere. If your parent leaves the house regularly, choose a mobile system.

2. Fall Detection — Is It Worth It?

Falls are the #1 cause of injury-related death in seniors over 65. Automatic fall detection means the system alerts responders even if your loved one is unconscious and can't press the button. We strongly recommend it for anyone who lives alone.

3. Monitoring Response Time

Response time varies dramatically between companies. In our testing, Medical Guardian averaged 32 seconds, while some budget services took over 90 seconds. In a cardiac emergency, those seconds matter enormously.

4. Contracts and Cancellation

Avoid any company requiring a long-term contract. All five of our top picks are month-to-month with no cancellation fees. This matters more than it sounds — your parent's needs may change, or a better product may become available. A 3-year contract that costs $200 to break is a significant financial trap.

5. Is Life Alert Worth It?

Life Alert is the most recognized brand in medical alert systems — their "I've fallen and I can't get up" commercials have run for decades. But brand recognition doesn't equal quality. Here's what our testing found compared to top alternatives:

  • Life Alert requires a 3-year contract, compared to month-to-month from all five of our top picks.
  • Life Alert's plan starts at $49.95/month — nearly double Bay Alarm Medical for equivalent features.
  • Response time averaged 46 seconds in our tests — slower than Medical Guardian (32s) and Bay Alarm (41s).
  • No family app — competitors like Medical Guardian include real-time tracking and alerts for family members at no extra cost.

Our recommendation: Medical Guardian or Bay Alarm Medical deliver better performance at lower cost with no contract. Life Alert's pricing and contract terms haven't kept pace with the competition.

6. Signal Range and Cellular Coverage

At-home base units typically cover 600–1,000 feet — enough for most homes and yards. Mobile systems depend on cellular networks. Medical Guardian and Medical Alert Classic use AT&T; Bay Alarm Medical uses both AT&T and Verizon (automatically selecting the stronger signal). Before purchasing, check coverage maps for your parent's address, and note which carrier the device uses. In rural areas, the dual-network Bay Alarm Medical has a meaningful advantage.

7. Total First-Year Cost

The advertised monthly rate is rarely the whole picture. Add up: equipment/device fee, activation fee, shipping, and the monthly plan for 12 months. Bay Alarm Medical charges $0 activation and free shipping; Medical Guardian charges $0–$149 depending on the package. Some companies advertise $19.95/month but add $99 in setup fees. Always ask for the full first-year cost in writing before committing.

8. Red Flags: What to Avoid

  • Contracts longer than month-to-month — the industry standard is now no contract required
  • Response time not publicly advertised — companies hiding this number typically have slow centers
  • Overseas monitoring centers — in a crisis, language barriers or background noise can delay help
  • No fall detection option — any credible provider should offer this, even as an add-on
  • High-pressure phone sales tactics — reputable companies let you research and decide on your own timeline
  • No money-back trial period — top companies offer 30-day returns; avoid any that don't

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my parent need a landline for a medical alert system?
No — all five of our top picks work on cellular networks with no landline required. This makes them much simpler to set up and use in any modern home.
How accurate is automatic fall detection?
In our testing, Medical Guardian detected 9/10 simulated falls. No system is 100% accurate — rapid sitting movements can trigger false alarms — but the best systems get it right the vast majority of the time.
Will Medicare cover a medical alert system?
Standard Medicare does not cover medical alert systems. However, some Medicare Advantage plans do offer an allowance. Check your specific plan's supplemental benefits, or contact your plan provider directly.
What happens if my parent presses the button by accident?
The monitoring center will speak with your parent through the device. If it's a false alarm, they simply say so and no emergency services are dispatched. There are no penalties for accidental activations.
Is Life Alert the best medical alert system?
No — Life Alert is the most advertised, not the best. In our testing, Medical Guardian had a faster response time (32 seconds vs. Life Alert's 46 seconds), costs less per month, and doesn't require a 3-year contract. Bay Alarm Medical also outperformed Life Alert at a lower price. Life Alert's brand recognition comes from decades of TV advertising, not test results.
What is the best medical alert system for a senior living alone?
Medical Guardian MGHome Cellular is our top recommendation for seniors who live alone. Its 32-second average response time is the fastest we tested, and automatic fall detection means help is dispatched even if your parent is unconscious and can't press the button. Fall detection is essential — not optional — for anyone living alone.
Can my parent wear a medical alert device in the shower?
Yes, and they should — the bathroom is where most senior falls happen. All five of our top picks are waterproof to at least IPX7 rating (submersible to 1 meter for 30 minutes). Medical Guardian, Bay Alarm Medical, and Lively Mobile Plus are all safe to wear in the shower every day.
How do I set up a medical alert system for my parent?
All five of our picks are designed for simple self-installation. Medical Guardian takes under 10 minutes: plug in the base unit, charge the wearable, and press the help button to run a test call with the monitoring center. Most companies offer a guided setup call with their support team at no extra charge. We recommend doing a test call together with your parent so they know exactly what to expect when they press the button in a real emergency.
