What to Look For in a Senior Phone

Button Size and Keyboard Layout

Seniors with arthritis or reduced dexterity need larger buttons with clear spacing. The Jitterbug Flip2 has the most senior-friendly physical keypad we tested — nearly 40% larger than a standard phone.

Speaker Volume and Clarity

Many seniors have some degree of hearing loss. Look for phones rated at 85+ dB speaker volume and hearing aid compatibility ratings of M3 or M4, and T3 or T4 for telecoil compatibility.

Emergency Features

A dedicated emergency button that bypasses the standard dialing process is crucial for seniors who may panic in an emergency. The Jitterbug's Urgent Response button is the gold standard here.

Screen Size and Text

For smartphones, look for displays 4.7" or larger with the ability to increase font size to "Extra Large" or "Accessibility" settings. The Jitterbug Smart3's 6.2" screen is particularly impressive for readability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest phone for a senior who has never used a cell phone?
The Jitterbug Flip2 is the easiest starting point. It works exactly like the simple phones of the early 2000s — open to answer, close to hang up — with no confusing apps or touchscreen required.
My parent wants to video call the grandkids. Which phone should I get?
The iPhone SE is the best for FaceTime with family. If they're already in the Apple ecosystem, it's seamless. Alternatively, the Jitterbug Smart3 supports video calling and is simpler to navigate.
Are Jitterbug phones worth the monthly plan cost?
Yes, for most seniors. The Lively plans start at $19.99/month and include the 24/7 Urgent Response feature, which provides peace of mind well beyond what a standard phone plan offers.
