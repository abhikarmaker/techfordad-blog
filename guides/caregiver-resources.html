Caregiver Resources

Support, tools, and resources to help you care for an aging parent without burning out.

Caring for a Parent Is Rewarding — and Exhausting

Caring for an aging parent is one of the most meaningful things you can do — and also one of the most demanding. The right mix of support resources and small technology wins can make a real difference in keeping both you and your parent healthy and connected.

What to Look For

• National caregiving organizations — many offer free guides, support groups, and helplines for family caregivers.
• Respite care services — short-term care that gives you a planned break, which research consistently shows helps prevent caregiver burnout.
• Care coordination apps — shared calendars and notes so siblings and family members can split up appointments, medications, and check-ins.
• Financial & legal planning — powers of attorney, advance directives, and benefits planning are easier to sort out before they're urgently needed.
• Local Area Agencies on Aging — a starting point for in-person resources like meal delivery, transportation, and in-home care in your area.
