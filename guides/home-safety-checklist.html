Home Safety Checklist for Seniors

A practical, room-by-room checklist to help an aging parent stay safe at home.

Most Accidents Happen in Predictable Places

Falls and accidents at home tend to happen in the same few spots — bathrooms, stairs, and poorly lit hallways. A short walkthrough of your parent's home using the checklist below can catch the most common hazards before they cause an injury.

What to Look For

• Bathroom — grab bars near the toilet and shower, a non-slip mat, and a shower chair if balance is a concern.
• Stairs & hallways — handrails on both sides, bright lighting, and no loose rugs or cords underfoot.
• Kitchen — frequently used items within easy reach so there's no need to climb on a stool.
• Bedroom — a nightlight or motion-activated light along the path to the bathroom, plus a phone or medical alert device within arm's reach.
• Whole home — working smoke and carbon monoxide detectors, and an emergency contact list posted somewhere visible.
