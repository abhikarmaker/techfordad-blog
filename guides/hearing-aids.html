Hearing Aids for Seniors

What families need to know about OTC hearing aids, fit, and getting a parent to actually wear them.

OTC Hearing Aids Changed the Game

Since over-the-counter (OTC) hearing aids became available without a prescription, treating mild-to-moderate hearing loss has gotten dramatically more affordable and accessible. That said, the right choice still depends on the severity of hearing loss, comfort, and whether your parent is willing to wear the device regularly.

What to Look For

• OTC vs. prescription — OTC devices suit mild-to-moderate loss; an audiologist fitting is best for moderate-to-severe loss.
• Rechargeable batteries — much easier for seniors with dexterity issues than tiny disposable batteries.
• Bluetooth streaming — lets hearing aids connect directly to a phone for calls and TV audio.
• Telecoil / hearing-aid compatibility — look for phones rated M3/M4 and T3/T4 (we note this in our cell phone reviews).
• Trial period & return policy — a real-world trial matters more than specs on paper.
