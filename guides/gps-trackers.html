GPS Trackers for Seniors

Peace of mind for families of seniors who wander, drive, or live alone.

Why Families Use GPS Trackers

GPS trackers for seniors come as wearable pendants, wrist bands, or shoe clips, and they help families keep an eye on a loved one who may wander due to dementia or simply want extra reassurance during walks, errands, or drives. Many medical alert systems now build GPS tracking directly into their mobile devices.

What to Look For

• Real-time vs. periodic updates — some trackers update location every few seconds, others check in every few minutes.
• Geofencing alerts — get a notification if your loved one leaves a set "safe zone," like home or a neighborhood.
• Battery life — daily charging is fine for some families, but multi-day battery life means fewer missed updates.
• Two-way calling or SOS button — lets your loved one call for help directly from the device.
• Monthly cost & coverage — most GPS trackers require a cellular data plan, typically $10–$20/month.
