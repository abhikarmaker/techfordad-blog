Fall Prevention Technology

Wearables, sensors, and smart devices that help prevent falls — and respond fast when they happen.

Falls Are the Leading Cause of Injury for Seniors

Falls are the leading cause of injury for adults 65 and older, but a combination of low-tech home fixes and modern devices can meaningfully reduce both the risk of falling and the time it takes to get help afterward.

What to Look For

• Automatic fall detection — built into many medical alert devices and smartwatches; it calls for help even if the wearer can't press a button.
• Smart motion sensors — can alert family members to unusual inactivity, which sometimes indicates a fall.
• Motion-activated lighting — reduces nighttime falls on the way to the bathroom.
• Grab bars & stability aids — low-tech, but still one of the most effective fall-prevention tools.
• Balance & strength programs — apps and community classes that build the strength to prevent falls in the first place.
